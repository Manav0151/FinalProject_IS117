Education.

John P. Stevens High School, located in Edison, NJ, was where I spent my high school years from September 2015 to June 2019. During my time there, I had the honor of serving as the captain of the baseball team. Through this position, I was able to develop my leadership skills while also representing the school in various tournaments and competitions. As I continued my education at the New Jersey Institute of Technology's Ying Wu College of Computing in Newark, NJ, I pursued a Bachelor of Arts in Information Systems, with an expected graduation date of May 2024. Throughout my academic journey, I have worked hard to maintain a high level of academic achievement, which has earned me the prestigious Diamond Club Organization Scholarship.

Activities.

Organized various volunteering events throughout my first three semesters. These included a food drive, a toy for tots gathering, and volunteering to do inventory at My Sister’s Closet.

Arranged many different activities for brothers of the fraternity to participate in, including service and brotherhood events.

Worked heavily with the fundraising committee to raise money for brothers and the community around us. Helped raise over $1200, mainly through social events and food reselling.

SOS Children's Villages Fundraiser raised over $5600 for less fortunate kids in Nairobi, Kenya to help provide them with essential materials to ensure a brighter future for vulnerable children.

Experience.

As an Assistant Manager at the Amazon Warehouse in Edison, NJ, I was responsible for assisting in the management of the largest and busiest warehouse in the United States. In this role, I provided guidance and directions to package handlers to expedite the process and ensure the efficient flow of operations. I was also tasked with controlling warehouse traffic throughout the facility to maintain a safe and organized environment. Through this experience, I developed strong leadership and communication skills while also gaining valuable experience in warehouse management.

During my time at Kumon Inc. in Edison, NJ, I worked as a Student Assistant and Grader, where I had the opportunity to work with students ranging from elementary to high school levels. I primarily focused on math and English subjects, and I developed a new oral section to help students practice their English skills and sharpen their mathematical abilities. This section was later implemented in almost every Kumon throughout our township. Through this experience, I gained valuable teaching and communication skills while also learning how to create and implement new educational programs.

As an Information Systems Intern at Russell Reynolds Associates in New York, NY, I worked on various projects using SQL scripting to streamline information based on company databases. I also worked closely with the Information Systems team to learn new skills such as SQL, Power BI, and Excel while furthering my communication skills altogether. This internship provided me with hands-on experience in the field of Information Systems, allowing me to apply my academic knowledge to real-world situations and gain valuable experience working in a professional environment.
